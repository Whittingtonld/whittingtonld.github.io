Installing Your New Bindings

Materials

Phillips Head Screwdriver
Snowboard
Bindings
8 Screws (included)
8 Washers(included)
Tape Measure
Snowboarding Boots

Time

Roughly 15-30 minutes
depending on how many adjustments are needed

Step 1. Setting up your Stance

Once your bindings are out of the box. Place them on your board over the holes in a comfortable stance. Place your bindings depending on your riding stance(Goofy/Regular). Use the picture on the right for guidance as to which foot goes first. Ratchets on bindings should face outside.( towards the nose and tail of the snowboard)
How do I find a Comfortable Stance?
If you have never ridden a snowboard a comfortable stance will typical be your feet a shoulder length apart. To make sure this stance is good for you, stand in your bindings. Adjust bindings either closer or farther apart until your stance feels comfortable. Make sure your bindings are over the holes in your board.

Step 2. Setting up your discs

Take the Binding discs and set them to your preferred angles. If you need help finding the right angles the video above is a great tool to see how.

Step 3. Screwing them onto the Board

Make sure the discs are still lined up with the holes in your snowboard.
1. Grab your Phillips head screw driver, four washers, and four screws
2. Place each screw into a washer
3. Place two screws(1,2) into the disc and into opposite holes
4. Partially thread screws 1 and 2
5. Insert the other two screws(3,4) in the remaining holes
6. Partially thread screws 3 and 4
7.  Hand tighten screws(1,2,3 and 4) until all the way in

After finishing this step

Step 4. Putting the screw covers on

Grab your screw cover and insert it into the middle of the disc. Your binding should look like the picture to the right when complete.

Step 5 Adjusting the Heelcup

You should adjust you heelcup to reduce your amount of toe drag.
1. Place your boot into your binding 
2. Your boot should look like the picture under the thumbs up
3. If adjustments are needed, loosen the heelcup screw on both sides
4. If your toe is too far back slide the heelcup in and if your toe is to far out pull your heelcup out.
5. After adjustment your boot should look like the picture under the thumbs up
6. Repeat step 4 until boot is in correct spot
7. Tighten both screws back up

Image Description

Step 6. Adjusting Your Ankle Strap

Ankle straps should be centered on the boot for ideal fit.
1. Place boot into the binding
2. To center the ankle strap, pop out the two metal studs connecting the strap to the ankle cushion
3. Center the cushion on your boot
4. Pop the metal studs into the strap in the required holes
5. Ankle strap should be centered, if not repeat steps 2-4

Step 7. Adjusting your Toe strap

Toe straps should be centered on the boot for ideal fit.
1. Place boot into the binding
2. To center the toe strap, loosen the toolless adjustment on the strap 
3. Center the strap on your toe
4. Tighten the toolless adjustment into the hole required.
5. Toe strap should be centered, if not repeat steps 2-4

Step 8. Go Shred

Every thing is done. Now its time to go enjoy the bindings.
